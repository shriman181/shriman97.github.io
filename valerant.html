Exhibit B — The platform

Four modules. One chain of custody.

Valerant covers the discovery pipeline from collection to production — every action timestamped, hashed, and logged for defensibility.

MOD.01

Relevance & issue scoring

Transformer-based models score every document against your matter's issue tags, with a confidence score and plain-language reasoning an attorney can verify in seconds — not re-derive from scratch.

MOD.02

Custodian data collection

Automated collectors pull from email, chat, cloud storage, and devices, preserving native metadata and generating a hash-verified collection manifest the moment data leaves the source.

MOD.03

Privilege log generation

Privilege candidates are flagged, classified by basis (attorney-client, work product, joint defense), and compiled into a draft log formatted for production — attorneys review and approve, not build from zero.

MOD.04

Chain of custody tracking

Every collection, transfer, review action, and redaction is recorded in an immutable audit log — exportable as a defensibility report for opposing counsel or the court.

Exhibit C — The workflow

From collection to production, in one pipeline.

Built around the seven-stage standard discovery pipeline — without the handoffs between five different vendors.

STAGE 1

Collect

Custodian sources are identified and pulled automatically with a verified manifest.

STAGE 2

Cull & dedupe

Date ranges, keywords, and custodians narrow the set before review begins.

STAGE 3

Score & flag

Relevance, privilege, and PII are scored with reasoning attached to each call.

STAGE 4

Review & approve

Attorneys confirm AI calls on the documents that matter most — nothing ships unreviewed.

STAGE 5

Privilege log

A formatted draft log is generated for every withheld or redacted document.

STAGE 6

Redact

PII and privileged content is redacted in-platform — no export to third-party tools.

STAGE 7

Produce

Final sets are exported in the format required by FRCP 34(b)(2)(E) and your protocol.

ALWAYS ON

Audit trail

Every step above writes to one immutable log, from first collection to final production.

Divanshu Garg founded Valerant after seeing how much of the cost in litigation comes down to people reading documents one at a time — and how little of that reading actually requires a law degree to get right the first time.

The thesis behind Valerant is narrow on purpose: instead of trying to replace legal judgment, build the layer underneath it. AI reads every document, scores it for relevance and privilege, drafts the paperwork that follows from those calls, and keeps a record of every decision — so the attorney's time goes to judgment calls, not data entry.

"A junior associate tagging documents at $500 an hour isn't practicing law — they're doing pattern matching the firm happens to bill at a lawyer's rate. Valerant does the pattern matching. The lawyer still makes the call."

Valerant is built with the defensibility requirements of real litigation in mind: every AI decision is explainable, every override is logged, and nothing leaves the platform without attorney sign-off. The goal isn't a black box that replaces review — it's a system that makes review fast enough that cost stops being the reason discovery gets scoped down.
